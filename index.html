History! (Oct 2000 image). My first website. A nationally touring Catholic/Lutheran band. I got this site by telling a friend his website was terrible and that I could do it better. Because I was not "employed" I had tons of freedom to try things. (podcasts, forums, membership model, ecommerce) Wrote my first perl/php code for Smallfish, spent some years booking the band.
Launched in 08, sold in 10. We worked with online professional poker player and built a subscriber base of ~600 people. Site was built on moveable type (wp alternative). We were small. I hated customer service. We had a chemistry, but both were happy to leave it.
October of 2013 my brother-in-law called me. *no notes...tell the story of how JRR started* Maza...closed because no power. He called me the next day...we need to support Jax local restaurants. Maybe we'll find a revenue model...maybe not.
Revenue model...we tried social media management...but we are bad at setting boundaries. Then we worked with Midori Sushi in Orange Park. May of 2014. Did a sushi making class. Selling tickets is hard work. We sold 8
Our first "guest" writer. Barbara Jeans seafood. Brittny Lowrey. June of 14. When we met her, she was and lived on the North Side. Good for 1 article per month. We had no revenue...so we "payed" her in restaurant invites.
August of '14 we did our first "food adventure". The location was a place called Tres Leches on Stockton. We had this elaborate plan for how the event would go. But my brother-in-law had to come late...his son ended up in the ER with a bloody nose that just wouldn't stop. We sold the place out! $29.99 for 6 courses. And we had foodies coming in and hugging us. We had all 39 seats sold... The meal took nearly 3 hours. But EVERYONE wanted to know where our next one was. Our 2nd was at a place called Dirty Reds...closed Our 3rd was at a place called Tapa That...closed
Jump to March of 2015. Our 10th foodie adventure. The first one I had to introduce. At this point my nephew was a patient at Duke University waiting for a stem cell transplant. I had to do the intro...we had TV coverage. It was a vegan event. We didn't know we had to limit ticket sales...sold 80 tickets. If you're vegan I'm not picking on you...but the eclectic group we had convinced the owner to add a vegan sushi menu.
May of 15...we'd built a rapport with Chef Rosaria Anderson. And we had our largest vegan event. 90 people. And my brother in law was late...he was driving back from NC. And I had to introduce the chef and the meal to 90 vegans.
June 20th of 15... carrot lox This was another vegan event...and this is when what we are doing "clicked" for me. Uptown Market closed on a Saturday for us... We sold every seat for a 6 course vegan food adventure. Brittny showed up...we were giving her and a friend a free meal. That event was a Saturday. The next day I flew to NC to see Eli in the hospital.
